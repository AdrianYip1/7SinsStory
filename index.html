Narrative Overview
The narrative of '7 Sins' centers on a main character who has had their traumatic memories erased by an entity. They embark on a journey to confront and defeat the seven deadly sins, each represented by a unique boss, to uncover the truth about their past and the sins that have affected those around them. The emotional tone is a mix of tension, introspection, and a quest for redemption.
Full Story Outline
The core theme of '7 Sins' revolves around the idea that confronting and understanding one's past traumas is crucial for personal growth and moving forward. The main character, after making a deal with the entity to erase their memories, must battle through various sins embodied by powerful bosses to help those affected by these sins. Each chapter focuses on a different sin, with the main character learning more about themselves and the impact of their actions as they progress. The overarching arc follows the main character's journey from a state of blissful ignorance to a reluctant acceptance of their past and the realization that confronting pain is necessary for true healing.

Main Character
You (Unnamed Protagonist)
As the unnamed protagonist of '7 Sins', you occupy a unique narrative role as a self-insert character, which inherently ties your journey to the player's own experiences and interpretations. This design choice allows for a deeply personal connection to the story, as players confront their own potential traumas and sins through your character. Your emotional throughline is shaped by a past filled with unresolved guilt and anger, stemming from a traumatic event that you have forgotten due to an external entity's intervention. This sets the stage for a journey of self-discovery, where you must navigate the complexities of confronting past traumas, battling sin-based entities, and learning that the path to healing is not always straightforward or guaranteed to bring happiness. Your ties to the core theme are evident in your belief that confronting pain and trauma is essential for moving forward, contrasting with the Entity's philosophy of erasing painful memories to alleviate suffering. Strongest Element: The strength of your character lies in the relatability and depth of your emotional journey. As a self-insert protagonist, you embody the player's own struggles with trauma and the desire for closure, making your experiences and growth resonate on a personal level. This deep emotional throughline not only drives the narrative forward but also engages the player in a meaningful way, as they see their own challenges and aspirations reflected in your character's story. Main Risk: The main narrative risk with your character as a self-insert protagonist is the potential for the story to become too personal or insular, potentially alienating players who do not share similar experiences or perspectives. There's also a risk that the emotional weight of your journey could lead to a sense of overwhelm or fatigue, especially if the story consistently presents darker and less certain resolutions to the conflicts you face. Immediate Fix: To improve the integration of the self-insert protagonist, consider introducing more diverse perspectives and experiences through the friend group and side characters. By ensuring that these characters have their own arcs and viewpoints, the narrative can explore a wider range of responses to trauma and sin, providing a more balanced and enriching story experience. This approach can help mitigate the risk of the story feeling too narrowly focused on your personal journey, while still allowing for the deep emotional engagement that is central to your character's design.
