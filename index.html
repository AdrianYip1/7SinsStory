Narrative Overview
The narrative revolves around the MC confronting their past traumas by battling entities representing the 7 deadly sins. The emotional tone is somber yet hopeful, emphasizing the importance of facing pain to achieve closure. The story is inspired by 'Persona 5', 'Omori', and 'Alan Wake 2', aiming for deep character interactions and psychological themes.
Full Story Outline
The core theme of '7 Sins' is the necessity of confronting and understanding one's pain to move forward. The overall arc follows the MC as they guide individuals overwhelmed by their sins to recognize and repent their wrongdoings. Each sin is represented by a boss character whose personal struggles and traumas drive their sinister behaviors. The MC's journey is one of personal growth, learning that while confronting pain is difficult, it is essential for true healing and happiness. The bosses' arcs typically involve a realization of their faults and a path towards redemption.

Focused read on protagonist strength and thematic clarity
Main Character
You (Unnamed Protagonist)
As the unnamed protagonist, you embody the role of a self-insert character who navigates a complex world filled with moral dilemmas and personal growth. Your narrative role is pivotal, as you are the conduit through which the themes of confronting versus avoiding trauma are explored. Your journey begins with unresolved guilt and a desire to escape from painful memories, aligning with the Entity's philosophy. However, through your interactions with various characters and bosses, you gradually shift towards valuing the importance of facing one's past. This emotional throughline is central to the story, reflecting the core theme that while pain is inevitable, confronting it is crucial for personal growth and moving forward. Your ties to the core theme are reinforced by your interactions with the Entity, the bosses, and your friends, each pushing you towards a deeper understanding of yourself and the world around you. Strongest Element: The strength of your character lies in its relatability and the depth of emotional engagement it offers. As a self-insert protagonist, players can easily project themselves into your shoes, making the narrative more immersive and personal. Your journey from avoidance to confrontation of trauma resonates with many, providing a powerful narrative that is both universal and deeply personal. Main Risk: The main narrative risk is that, as a self-insert character, you might not stand out enough as a distinct protagonist. Without a strong, unique identity separate from the player's projection, you risk becoming a generic vessel for the story, lacking the depth and development needed to captivate and engage the audience on a broader scale. Immediate Fix: To improve the integration of the self-insert protagonist, introduce more distinct personality traits, backstory elements, and decision-making moments that set you apart from the player. This could include unique interactions with the Entity and the bosses, showcasing your growth and development as a character, rather than just a reflection of the player's journey.
