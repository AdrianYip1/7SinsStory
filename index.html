Narrative Overview
The narrative revolves around a protagonist grappling with the consequences of avoiding trauma, guided by an external entity that seeks to erase painful memories. The story is dark and introspective, focusing on the struggle between confronting pain and the desire for its erasure. Emotional tones range from confusion and frustration to empathy and eventual resolution.
Full Story Outline
The core theme of '7 Sins' is the conflict between confronting and avoiding pain, embodied in the protagonist's journey against entities representing the 7 deadly sins. The protagonist, burdened by past traumas, makes a deal with an entity to forget his suffering. As he battles sin entities, he learns that confronting pain, though difficult, leads to growth. Each sin entity represents a different coping mechanism, with the protagonist helping individuals realize their truths and move forward. The journey culminates in a confrontation with the entity itself, challenging the protagonist's beliefs about pain and memory.

Main Character
You (Unnamed Protagonist)
As the unnamed protagonist, you play a pivotal role in navigating the complex world of '7 Sins'. Your narrative role is that of a healer and a seeker of truth, representing the belief that confronting past traumas is essential for personal growth and moving forward. Your journey begins with a deal to forget your traumatic memories, which sets the stage for your exploration of the world and its various sins. Your agency is significant, as you are the one who confronts and battles the manifestations of the 7 Sins, learning from each encounter and growing more aware of your own emotional landscape. The emotional throughline of your character is one of struggle and redemption, marked by unresolved guilt and anger stemming from past events, which you gradually confront and resolve. Your ties to the core theme are evident in your approach to dealing with trauma, which contrasts with the Entity's belief in forgetting. You embody the idea that facing pain head-on is crucial for obtaining closure and achieving peace. Your character is designed to evolve throughout the story, starting from a place of denial and avoidance, to one of acceptance and understanding. This evolution is mirrored in the changing resolutions of the battles you face, from initially positive outcomes to more complex and bittersweet conclusions. Your interactions with the bosses and their backstories further illuminate your own journey, as you see reflections of your struggles in their stories. Your relationship with the Entity also plays a crucial role in your character development. Initially, you accept the Entity's offer to forget your pain, but as you progress through the story, you begin to question this decision. This conflict between the Entity's philosophy and your own beliefs forms a central part of your character arc, leading to the final confrontation where you must decide whether to continue opposing the Entity. Your progression through the story is marked by a deepening understanding of the complexities of human emotions and the consequences of avoiding or confronting them. This understanding is not only personal but also extends to your interactions with the bosses and their associated characters, as you help them realize their own truths and come to terms with their pasts. Strongest Element: Your strongest current element is your ability to empathize with the bosses and their situations, which allows you to connect with them on a deeper level and facilitate their realization and redemption. This empathy not only aids in the resolution of their arcs but also enriches your own character development by exposing you to a wide range of human experiences and emotions. Main Risk: One main narrative risk is the potential for your character to become a passive recipient of events rather than an active agent in shaping the story. Given your role as a self-insert protagonist, there is a risk that your actions and decisions could be perceived as too predictable or lacking in depth, which might diminish the overall impact of your character arc. Immediate Fix: To improve the integration of the self-insert protagonist, consider adding more layers to your character's backstory and motivations, making your decisions and actions more nuanced and less formulaic. This could involve exploring additional conflicts or challenges that are unique to you, which would not only make your character more relatable but also enhance the overall narrative by providing fresh perspectives on the themes of the story.
